Osman Hamdi Bey

Here's a time line of Osman Hamdi Bey's life:

  • 1842 - Born in Istanbul, Ottoman Empire
  • 1860 - Leaves for Paris to study Law.
  • 1862 - Decides to pursue his interest in painting instead, lefts the Law program, and traines under French orientalist painters.
  • 1869 - Marries his first wife of Marie, a French woman whom he met in Paris when he was a student.
  • 1881 - Is assigned to the Imperial Museum as the director, develops the museum, rewrites the antiquities laws, and creates nationally sponsored archaeological expeditions.
  • 1884 - Oversees the promulgation of a Regulation prohibiting historical artifacts from being smuggled abroad, a giant step in constituting a legal framework of preservation of the antiquities.
  • 1887 - Conducts the first scientific based archaeological researches done by a Turkish team. His digs includeds sites as varied as the Commagene tomb-sanctuary in Nemrut Dağı in southeastern Anatolia which is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • 1891 - Opens the Istanbul Archaeology Museum to lodge the pieces discovered during his excavations.
  • 1906 - Paints the famous painting, The Tortoise Trainer, which holds the record for the most valuable Turkish painting, after being sold for 3.5 million dollars in December 2004.
  • 1910 - Dies at the age of 67.
  • Paintings - His works bear witness to a patient and conscientious method, and may be considered as documents of art history. He was the first who dared break with the Turkish pictorial tradition.
